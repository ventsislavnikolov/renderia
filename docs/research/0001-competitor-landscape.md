## Verdict on named claims

### Habitas — EXISTS, and matches the claimed feature set almost exactly
**habitas.ai** is a real, live product: "Habitas — AI home design", tagline "Room Designs in Seconds with AI" (https://habitas.ai). Verified verbatim from the homepage:

- **One photo, ~1 minute**: "One photo. That's all it takes." / "Three steps. One minute." — generates **3 style variants** in parallel, ~60s per variant.
- **Structure preserved**: "Three distinct styles, your exact room structure." Locks walls, windows, doors; changes only furniture/materials/styling.
- **Real product references**: taste **input** (Pinterest boards / product links you feed in), not verified as an output catalog of purchasable products with prices.
- **Execution PDF**: "Get the execution plan: What to buy, what to change, difficulty ratings." / "Hand your contractor a real plan."
- **Sharing**: share the project with partner/designer/contractor.
- **Pricing**: Free ($0 — 1 project, 3 rooms, 2 AI designs), Starter $12/mo, Pro $29/mo.

Caveats: invisible in generic search (low SEO, web-only, no app-store presence found); the name is dominated by the **Our Habitas** hotel brand (https://www.ourhabitas.com/). Feature-level it is the closest overlap with Renderia's positioning. Apparent gaps vs Renderia: no multi-angle coherent concepts, no priced product output, no budget comparison.

### Scenli — NOT FOUND
Search variants ("Scenli", "Scenli AI interior design", domain checks scenli.com/scenli.ai) returned nothing. Nearest collisions are unrelated (Scrnli screenshot tool, Scenly language app, Scenio AI image gen). **No product called Scenli exists in the AI interior space as of 2026-07 — claims attributed to it are unsubstantiated.**

## Planner 5D
Sources: https://planner5d.com/use/ai-interior-design · https://planner5d.com/use/ai-room-design · https://planner5d.com/use/ai-floor-plan-generator

- AI recognition of walls/doors/windows from photos/plans → editable 3D model: yes.
- Editable 2D/3D (~8,000-item library), VR walkthroughs, collaboration on paid plans: yes.
- Nuance: output is a **3D-modeled reconstruction**, not a photoreal restyle of the actual photo. Shopping lists are generic catalog cost estimates, not retailer SKUs. No contractor document. Freemium + subscription.

## IKEA Kreativ
Sources: IKEA newsroom + knowledge base + Retail Technology Innovation Hub (Dec 2024).

- Scene Scanner builds an interactive replica of the room; Eraser Tool removes existing furniture; places real IKEA catalog items only; save/share and buy directly; free.
- Reported live in **29 IKEA markets** (excluding China), web + app; 3M+ users per Ingka. Exact country list: UNVERIFIED.
- Limits: IKEA-only catalog, no contractor document, furniture-swap focus rather than renovation of finishes/architecture.

## What differentiation remains

**Commoditized (don't lead with these):**
- "From your own photo, fast" — table stakes.
- Structure preservation *as a claim* — ReimagineHome, HomeDesigns.AI, Spacely, Habitas, Renovate AI all market it by name. Differentiation now lives in *how well* (radiators, sockets, sloped ceilings), a quality claim needing proof.
- Shoppable real products with prices — several tools have it.
- Sharing — universal.

**Genuinely open:**
1. **Contractor/execution document** — only tiny Habitas claims it; near-white space, but no longer unique. Speed matters.
2. **Multi-angle coherent concepts** — nobody claims per-angle consistency of one concept across photos of the same room; every competitor is single-image-in/out. Renderia's strongest technical moat candidate.
3. **Concept-level budget comparison** — per-item costs exist (Renovate AI); side-by-side concept budgets as a decision tool do not.
4. **The "decision workspace" chain itself** — coherent concepts → priced multi-retailer plan → contractor handoff as one workflow is offered by no one verified. ReimagineHome's "Design clarity you can act on" shows the market drifting toward decision language — the window is open but narrowing.

**Strategic notes:** monitor habitas.ai directly (closest overlap, minimal traction); discard anything citing "Scenli".
